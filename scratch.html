✦ A birthday letter, straight from the heart
Dear Reshma,

Happy Birthday. Two words that feel so small for everything I actually want to say to you today. But here I am, trying anyway — because you deserve to hear it, all of it, on a day that belongs entirely to you.

I still remember the day we met — September 12, 2023. A mutual friend, a casual introduction, nothing planned. I had no idea that the person standing in front of me that day would become one of the most important people in my life. Funny how the universe works like that. It doesn't announce the moments that will change everything. It just quietly places them in front of you.

There are people you meet and forget. And then there are people who quietly become the reason you believe in good things again. You are that person for me. You didn't just walk into my life — you filled it. The kind of warmth you carry, the way you show up without being asked, the way you make someone feel seen without even trying — I don't think you understand how rare that is. How rare you are. There are days I don't say it, days I probably should have — but every single day, in some quiet corner of my heart, I am grateful you exist. Grateful that out of everyone in this world, I get to call you mine.

I owe you an apology. A real one — not the kind said quickly to move past something, but the kind that means I've sat with it and felt the weight of it. I fought with you over things that didn't deserve a fight. I made small things into storms. And worse — there were moments that were serious, moments you needed me to be present and gentle, and I was careless instead. I was playful when you needed me to listen. I brushed off things that mattered to you, and I know that hurt. I know I hurt you. And I am so, deeply sorry for that. You never deserved any of it.

And if I am being truly honest with you today — it was never just you. I have hurt everyone I truly care about. Knowingly, unknowingly, with my playfulness, with my carelessness, with my inability to be serious when it mattered the most. The people closest to me — the ones I would never want to lose — are the ones I have somehow hurt the deepest. And I carry that. I carry the weight of knowing that I have been the worst version of myself to the very people who deserved the best of me. I don't say this so you feel sorry for me. I say it because I want you to know that I see it. I see what I have done. And I am asking you,genuinely, from the bottom of my heart — please forgive me. For every moment I made you feel unheard, dismissed, or like your feelings were too much or your not enough. You were never too much. I was just too careless. And I promise you — I give you my word — that I will never let this happen again. Not with you. Never again with you.

So today, on your birthday, I want you to feel everything you always give to others — warmth, joy, the feeling of being truly seen. I want this day to be soft and beautiful and full of everything that makes you smile. You give so much of yourself to the people around you. Today, please let yourself receive.

Happy Birthday, Reshma. Thank you for being my person. Thank you for staying. Thank you for being exactly who you are — because who you are is someone worth celebrating every single day. 🌸✨

With all my heart —
Your Rakshith 💛
